    Applied Fish Ecology

    My research emphasizes ecological principles to identify abiotic and biotic factors that influence population dynamic rates and aquatic assemblage structure. I incorporate my interest in ecological dynamics with my comprehensive training in fisheries management to contribute practical solutions to the complex threats facing diverse aquatic ecosystems. My research program exists at the threshold between aquatic ecology and its direct applications to management and restoration. By testing ecological hypotheses, I generate basic knowledge about species, populations, and entire aquatic communities which can be applied using stakeholder derived management objectives and action alternatives.
