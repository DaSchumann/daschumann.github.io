## TARGETED CONSERVATION INVESTMENTS: A STRATEGIC DECISION FRAMEWORK FOR FLOODPLAIN DEPENDENT FISHES IN THE LOWER MISSISSIPPI RIVER ***
####**Objectives** 1. **Targeted regional conservation investments.** We are identifying important regions and configurations for future easement acquisitions in floodplain habiats 2. **Sharing the floodplain.** We are forecasting the outcomes of proposed management actions that impact the timing and duration of floodplain inundation events for aquatic and terrestrial taxa that depend on these habitats
####**Overview** Access to floodplain habitats by dependent fishes is limited by anthropogenic modifications of the Lower Mississippi River to facilitate navigation and commerce. Modifications were, in part, done to prevent flooding of agricultural and private lands, but have degraded water quality, habitat heterogeneity, fish assemblage structure. Today, fewer floodplain areas are inundated less frequently in channelized reaches of the Lower Mississippi River. Land acquisition by management agencies and other organizations can potentially increase floodplain habitat if river connectivity is restored. However, resources to acquire lands are finite and if multiple acquisition opportunities exist then a framework to identify opportunities that maximize the likelihood of achieving stakeholder objectives are needed. Restoration of floodplain dependent fishes, with focus on Alligator Gar, in the Lower Mississippi River can be enhanced by identifying priority locations for future easements and describing best management practices for new properties in the context of stakeholder objectives. We are engaging federal, state, NGO, private, and recreational stakeholders in the region to improve floodplain habitats for fish reproduction.
