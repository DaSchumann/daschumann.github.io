Applied Fish Ecology

My research emphasizes ecological principles and relies on my comprehensive training in fisheries management to deliver applied solutions to the complex threats facing aquatic ecosystems. I am primarily interested in the unique management challenges posed in rivers and streams and the role of animal movement behaviors and species interactions on community dynamics.

By identifying abiotic and biotic factors that influence population dynamic rates and aquatic assemblage structuring I support management of sportfishes and diverse aquatic taxa alike, describe threats of invasive species establishment, and improve restoration efforts degraded habitats and rare species. My research program exists at the threshold between aquatic ecology and its direct applications to management and restoration. By testing ecological hypotheses, I generate basic knowledge about species, populations, and entire aquatic communities which can be applied using stakeholder derived management objectives and action alternatives.
