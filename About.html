Applied Fish & Aquatic Ecology

My research program exists at the threshold between community ecology and its direct applications to management and restoration. By testing ecological hypotheses, I generate knowledge about species, populations, and entire aquatic assemblages which can be applied to address stakeholder derived management objectives and assess conservation actionalternatives.

I am primarily interested in the unique management challenges posed by river and stream systems, but also investigate the role of aquatic invasive species and movement behaviors on conservation effectiveness. I conduct research and develop conservation planning tools that are used to support decision making for diverse taxa.
